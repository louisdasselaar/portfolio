My Projects

“Gait Guard” Ankle Bracelet

During my studies, I collaborated on this project with an engineering friend. Together, we invented a device that can provide autonomous haptic feedback to people with knee arthritis.

We have demonstrated that it is possible to develop an ankle accessory that utilizes AI to make patients aware of their walking patterns. This has the potential to contribute to reducing knee pain and improving walking patterns.

We successfully completed the project with a score of 8.2 out of 10 and continued our work in our spare time.

Connectivity Logger

In my second year of Electrical Engineering at Hogeschool Utrecht, I interned at Sodaq as a Hardware Engineer. My task was to create a "connectivity logger" to test the range of both LTE-M and GPS signals within a building.

My approach involved designing hardware that could be integrated into an existing Sodaq product. I was responsible for both hardware and software development, designing and implementing both aspects.

“Active” One-Arm Wheelchair

During my studies, I collaborated with mechanical engineers on a significant project—an 'active' one-arm wheelchair designed for individuals paralyzed on one side of their body. Recognizing the importance of maintaining muscle activity for people in wheelchairs, we aimed to create a solution that promotes movement.

The device is modular, easily attachable to compatible wheelchairs, and operates by manipulating the handle. I designed the functionality of this handle, which seamlessly transitions from neutral to active mode upon detecting a person's hand. The side panel features directional buttons, allowing users to select a direction before moving the handle to initiate motion.
